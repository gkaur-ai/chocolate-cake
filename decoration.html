DECORATING THE CAKE

he earliest “cakes” were made by the Egyptians, but they were more like sweetened bread, often flavoured with honey, nuts, and dried fruits. The idea of the fluffy sponge cake we know today didn’t arrive until centuries later, when baking techniques improved. The word “cake” comes from the Old Norse word kaka. Vikings used it to describe small baked goods. This term travelled through languages and centuries to become the “cake” we use today. It’s a slice of history we can’t skip when sharing fun facts about cakes. One of the most surprising facts about cake is that the earliest chocolate cakes were not sweet at all. They were closer to spicy or bitter breads infused with cocoa. Sweet chocolate cakes only came into the picture after sugar became widely available and chocolate itself evolved from a bitter drink to the sweet treat we love now. In 2008, bakers in Indonesia created a cake that stood over 108 feet tall. That’s taller than a ten-storey building! The cake was made to celebrate the anniversary of their shopping mall and took days to assemble. Imagine trying to cut the first slice – you’d need a crane.

Step1

Place one cake layer on a cake board or plate. Add desired amount of frosting to the top of the layer and spread evenly.

Step2

Continue to repeat this process until the layers are stacked.

Step3

Spread a very thin layer of frosting all over the cake (crumb coat). Chill your cake for 30 minutes until the buttercream is firm to the touch.

Step4

Frost the cake using the buttercream. For smooth results, use an offset spatula with a bench scraper.

Step5

Chill cake for another 30 minutes.

Step6

Take slightly cooled chocolate ganache and gently drizzle down the sides of the cake and spread it over the top.

Step7

Using a pastry bag with a 1M piping tip, create swirls of buttercream around the top edges and bottom edges of the cake.
